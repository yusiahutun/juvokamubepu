Cher overreacts after Twitter fan claims Nicki Minaj 'dissed' her in rap song

'I've seen lots of people come and go': Cher overreacts after Twitter fan claims Nicki Minaj 'dissed' her in rap song

By Marcus Barnes
Updated: 14:38, 28 November 2011

66

comments

She may be 65-years-old and one of the music world's best known performers, but that hasn't stopped Cher from overreacting to a fan's comments on her Twitter page.

Thanks to the social networking site, the singer ended up at loggerheads with rap star Nicki Minaj after a fan instigated the clash.

Nicki, 28, was accused by one of Cher's supporters of 'dissing' the singer in a song from her album Pink Friday.

Twitter beef: Nicki Minaj and Cher were pitted against each other in a battle on the social networking site

A Cher fan alerted the singer to Minaj's track Did It On 'Em saying: '@cher did you know that b***h @NICKIMINAJ dissed you in her song DID IT ON EM.'

To which Cher confidently replied: 'Ive seen lots of people come & go! No biggie !'

However she soon felt the wrath of 'Team Minaj', with fans coming out in defense of the rap superstar and saying Cher should listen to the song before responding.

Unnecessary: A tweet from one fan started a whole pointless exchange - mainly involving Scaff Beezy and Cher

In the song, Minaj raps: 'If you could turn back time, Cher/ You used to be here, now you’re gone, Nair.'

It was explained that the lyrics were merely a rap metaphor and not a jab at Cher herself.

Minaj's alleged boyfriend Scaff Beezy soon got himself involved in the war of words.

Acceptance: Cher acknowledges that that she was in the wrong

He wrote: 'yo @cher listen to the words b4 you say dumb s**t on twitter… do you know what a rap metaphore is??? TM is a cult you dont want it with!!'

Eventually though Cher admitted defeated and accepted she was in the wrong.

She wrote: 'U R right ! Someone said I was dissed..I’ve been dissed b4,but instead of finding out. ..I just got defensive ! I should know better ! Dumb.'

Short and sweet: Nicki Minaj's contribution to the debate was minimal to say the least

Her tweets were then deleted from her profile.

In the midst of all this, Minaj only had one quick tweet to offer once she learned about what was going on.

She simply wrote: '@Cher #stopit5.'

It's not the first time Cher has lost her cool on her Twitter page.

In January she lashed out at a fan over Burlesque being overlooked in the Best Original Song category for the 83rd Academy Awards.

A day after nominations were announced, one fan wrote: 'Cher Gripes about not getting nominated ... Would you like some cheese to go with that whine?!"'

Cher wrote back: 'Whatever your name is, it is with total disregard and complete lack of respect ... that I wish you goodnight and good fkn luck.'
