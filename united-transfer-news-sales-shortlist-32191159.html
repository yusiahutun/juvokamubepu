Man Utd could 'put 11 players up for sale' as Sir Jim Ratcliffe transfer revolution begins

The first summer transfer window of Sir Jim Ratcliffe's Manchester United reign looks sure to be a busy one.

But the new investor's first call to order may not be a glamourous as many fans were hoping following his £1billion purchase of a 27.7% stake at Old Trafford. That's after it emerged trimming the wage bill will be a chief priority to make way for a fresh wave of talent at the Theatre of Dreams.

ESPN reported Ratcliffe and INEOS sport director Dave Brailsford have come to the conclusion the wage bill is in need of reform if they're to realise their vision of a United overhaul. It's believed the club won't be able to make invest as desired without falling afoul of the Premier League's Profit and Sustainability Rules (PSR) as potential being docked points, and as many as 11 players may face the axe to bring things back into balance.

Top earners like Raphael Varane, Casemiro, Harry Maguire, Christian Eriksen and Victor Lindelof could be made available to potential suitors, while Aaron Wan-Bissaka and Scott McTominay are also touted for possible exits. Anthony Martial will also clear £250,000 a week off the wage bill when his contract expires in June.

On top of that, it's understood those already out on loan could be shortlisted for sales in the summer. That contingent includes Jadon Sancho (Borussia Dortmund), Facundo Pellistri (Granada), Donny van de Beek (Eintracht Frankfurt) and Brandon Williams (Ipswich).
