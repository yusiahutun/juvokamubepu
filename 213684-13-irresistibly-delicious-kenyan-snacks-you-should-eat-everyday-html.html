Top Kenyan snacks that you should try out

Kenya has diverse cuisines and cultures and Kenyans have developed a liking for particular snacks sold in many urban centres especially in Nairobi

They are made in not so hygienic standards but these snacks are loved by everybody.

You will see Kenyans in urban centres especially in Nairobi nibbling on these irresistible snacks at any time of the day especially in the evening during rush hours.

1. Roasted maize

Perhaps the most popular snack found in almost  every corner of urban centres.

It goes down very well if its smeared with a generous amount of lemon, salt and pepper. Mahindi choma as it is popularly known can be eaten anytime but its sweeter in the evening as you sit inside a matatu stuck in traffic!

Never mind that those who roast maize do not have the cleanest of hands and by the way do they wash after visiting the loo or handling money?

2. Mutura (African Sausage)

Another very popular snack mostly cooked in the evening by the vendors, for unkown reasons.

Mutura is made by stuffing goat or cow intestines with tiny pieces of meat which have been cooked with or without pepper added. This is then grilled over a charcoal fire.

There is nothing as sweet as the aroma of  cooking mutura. There is noting as tantalizing as eating well made mutura. Never mind where the meat came from.

3. Roasted chicken necks,gizzards and liver

We are used to the whole chicken without its other parts, but have you ever wondered where some of the other chicken parts are taken to after slaughter? Well, let me enlighten or rather surprise you by telling you that they are not thrown away as you would have assumed.

Very yummy! Roast chicken necks, gizzards and liver can be found in various local joints, Ask Kahawa Wendani guys!

4. Samosa

Perhaps the oldest Kenyan snack which Mombasa women know how to make well. The triangular shaped snacks can be stuffed with minced meat, cow peas and waru for our Kikuyu brothers and sisters. Samosas can go any time of the day.

One good thing about samosa is that you can have one with pepper for those who don’t mind or better still one cooked without it for those who wouldn’t want to spoil the good taste with some bitterness. But either way, you choose to have your samosa, it’s a great local snack.

5. Boiled egg and kachumbari

You do not have to worry about your daily intake of proteins as boiled eggs are being hawked everywhere nowadays. They come with a good serving of kachumbari made of sliced tomatoes, onions and pepper.

In Kenya, guys selling these eggs are strategically positioned to attract the right customers. You will find them at the stage where you board or alight from the matatu. Alternatively, you can find them along the streets, ready to serve you. The cost-friendly price charged for one makes it easier to buy and sell.

6. Roasted yams/sweet potatoes

In the past, people were considering yams as a food suitable for a particular community, especially where they are grown. But as we speak now, things are far much different.

Kenyans are now becoming health conscious and are now going back to the roots literally, eating traditional foods.

You will now see roasted yams or sweet potatoes on sale at the roadside. They are very healthy local snacks to bite on the way.

7. Boiled green maize

It is now becoming a very common snack of late and nothing beats nibbling on a boiled cob with a sprinkling of salt.

If you are lucky enough to get hold of boiled soft- maize, you will attest to the fact that the taste is fantastic.

8. Ugali fries

When the word fries is mentioned, all you can think of is potatoes. Well, there is some new snack in town for the lovers of fries. Gone are the days you wouldn't want to take a piece of ugali for breakfast. When you go to the highly-rated hotels, on the list of appetizers is ugali fries.

The coating gives the ugali an excellent crunch that you won't remember its ugali you are having. Besides, you have the liberty of spicing it up with whatever spices you prefer as you make the coating.

9. Nyama choma

How will you prove to people that you have been to Kenya? Forget the Maasai shukas and the locally-made bracelets; Nyama Choma is the real deal.

Nyama Choma is roasted meat; either cow, goat or sheep meat. You can have Nyama Choma alone or accompanied with an accompaniment of your choices such as Ugali or chapatti. You can take it in the morning, lunchtime or even supper and well, don't forget to ask for kachumbari while you are at it.

10. Lemon-spiced mandazi

Mandazis are one of the most commonly taken snacks in Kenya. They are also readily available in Kenyan restaurants as well as by the roadsides.

The addition of lemon to spice up the mandazi has been one of the highest inventories as it has given the mandazi a whole new flavor. If you choose to have you mandazi from the roadside, you don't have to worry about tea. In most cases, the friendly sellers will have a tea flask readily available for you.

11. Kenyan mutton kebabs

While in Kenya, you don't have to worry about what snack to take because the list is endless. The dilemma will be on what to choose and what to leave. As you are struggling with the indecisiveness, try out the mutton kebab.

The kebab is made up of minced mutton with an egg coating. Adding the spices and herbs of your choice can take your experience and taste to a whole new level.

12. Egg rolls

Are you tired of the monotonous boiled eggs with kachumbari? Are you wondering how else you can take your boiled eggs without getting bored? The African egg roll is a perfect choice.

The egg roll is made by coating the boiled egg with breadcrumbs, flour and dipping them in beaten egg before deep frying. You can add different spices to the meal to make them tastier.

13. Pack potatoes

I know the name sounds a bit uncommon, but guess what, these are viazi karai. When you visit the coastal region, you will find people selling viazi karai at every corner. You don't have to go to Mombasa to enjoy this snack as you can also find it all over the country.

There are made of boiled potatoes which are filled with a sour paste coated with different types of batter before deep frying. Once you spot viazi karai being cooked near you, be sure to grab a plate, and you will attest to the fact that you can't have enough of them.
