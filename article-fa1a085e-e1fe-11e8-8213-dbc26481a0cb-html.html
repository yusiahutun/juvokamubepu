Sadie Leather Ainsworth Smith | Obituaries

Sadie Leather Ainsworth Smith was born May 4, 1941 in Jones County. She was a longtime member of Grace Baptist Church and attended Mill Creek Church.

Visitation will be 4-8 p.m. Sunday at Memory Chapel. Funeral services will be at 2:30 p.m. Monday at Mill Creek Church with visitation at the church at 1 p.m. Brother Bill Myers will officiate.

Sadie was always young at heart with a bubbly personality that never met a stranger. She always had a smile for someone. She was a stay-at-home mother and wife who cared for and loved her family. She enjoyed spending time with her family, especially during the holidays. She loved Christmas and would have decorations from top to bottom of her house.

Sadie was loved by her family and friends. She was called “Mawmaw” by her grandchildren and great-grands, who looked forward to hearing her stories and Eskimo kisses.

She will be missed by many.

She was preceded in death by her husband of 62 years Marvin G. Smith; parents Fred T. Ainsworth and Stella Ree Ward Ainsworth; son Danny Lee Smith Sr.; grandson Danny “DJ” Lee Smith Jr.; brothers Fred Rae Ainsworth and Ernie Ainsworth; sisters Ermia Knight, Sandra K. Mozingo and Melody Manaco.

Survivors include her daughters Barbara Jean Smith Saul (Michael) of the Rustin community and Cindy Marie Smith Williams (David) of Pearl; daughter-in-law Teresa Smith of Laurel; grandchildren Karen Elizabeth Irving McFetridge (William), Angela Brianne Saul Dial, Wilson David Williams, Wesley Reagan Williams, John Marvin Smith “Johnny” and Christopher William Smith; step-grandson Jeffery Michael Haun Smith; great-grandchildren Anna Elizabeth McFetridge, William Cody McFetridge, Danny Wade Dial Jr., Brianna Gean Dial, Courtney Ray Dial, Joshua Alton Smith, Hailey Nicole Smith, Danny Lee Smith III and Kimberlyn Brooklyn Smith; great-great-grandchild Kayden Lane Smith; brothers Tilson Earl Ainsworth of Kiln and Roy Ainsworth (Lucinda) of Slidell, La.; sisters Linda Chambers of Slidell and Brenda Paugh of Brighton, Colo.; and a host of nieces and nephews, great-nieces and great-nephews, and a multitude of cousins and family members.

Pallbearers will be Randy Smith, William McFetridge, John M. Smith, Wesley Williams, Tony Smith and Vinson Hoover. Honorary pallbearers will be Joshua Smith and Eugene Smith.

To sign the online guestbook, visit www.memorychapellaurel.com.
