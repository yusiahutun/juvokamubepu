Gulf Energy was one of the oil marketing companies that bagged a tender to import fuel from the Middle East under a government-to-government deal.

The petroleum company was tasked with importing three consignments of petrol, two diesel cargoes, and one jet fuel shipment monthly.

According to Business Daily, Gulf Energy imports between 250,000 tonnes and 350,000 tonnes of super monthly and an estimated 70,000 tonnes to 200,000 tonnes of diesel. It also ships in 80,000 tonnes of jet fuel per month.

Who owns Gulf Energy?

Rubis acquired a controlling stake in Gulf Energy in 2019 in a deal worth KSh 16.4 billion, as approved by the Competition Authority of Kenya.

Rubis Energy Kenya is owned by Rubis Energie, a subsidiary of the Rubis Group, listed on the Paris Stock Exchange in France.

The transaction involved the acquisition of the entire issued share capital of Gulf by Rubis Energy. It qualified as a merger within the meaning of sections 2 and 41 of the Competition Act No.12 of 2010.

According to data from the Registrar of Companies, as reported by Citizen Digital, the directors of Gulf Energy are listed as Paul Kiprotich Limo and Francis Koome Njogu.

Mombasa businessman Suleiman Shahbal had a 25% stake in Gulf Energy through his company, Monte Carlo Investments Limited, Who Owns Kenya reported.

Auron Energy which is listed in Mauritius, is another shareholder of the oil company.

Why does the government import fuel on credit?

The government-to-government deal was announced by Energy Cabinet Secretary (CS) Davis Chirchir on Monday, March 13, at Kawi House in Nairobi.

Under the agreement, Aramco was contracted to supply diesel cargo consignments while the Abu Dhabi National Oil Company was tasked with supplying petrol.

Chirchir announced that the deal paved the way for Kenya to pay for fuel in shillings, easing the pressure on the local currency, which was on a free fall against the United States (US) dollar.

"The product will now be paid for in Kenyan shillings, and this will ensure the dollar is available for other sectors of the economy," Chirchir assured.

President William Ruto projected the Kenyan shilling would strengthen to 120 or 115 against the dollar following the deal, but this failed.

"My economic adviser David Ndii, Mohammed Hassan, Davis Chirchir and the young man at EPRA (Daniel Kiptoo) have done something phenomenal. They have managed to put together a programme that has taken us away from looking for 500 million dollars every month to buy fuel Today in our country, we can buy our fuel in KSh. This will remove the pressure on our dollars. In the next coming months, in my estimation, the dollar exchange rate will come below KSh 120, maybe KSh 115," Ruto explained on Tuesday, April 11.

On Friday, November 24, the shilling exchanged at 152.81 against the dollar, according to an official rate by the Central Bank of Kenya (CBK).

Who owns Galana Energies Ltd?

Galana Energies was also another company that secured a deal to import fuel.

It owns a chain of petrol stations under the Delta brand. Anthony Munyasya, the chief executive officer, heads the oil marketing firm.

Galana Energies Ltd was registered on June 7, 2000, and is owned by three firms. These are Sai Ram Investment Company (1,576 shares), Romichi Company (1,576 shares) and Tapiola Limited (1,598 shares).
