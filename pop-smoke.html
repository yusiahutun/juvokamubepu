Biography Timeline

Pop Smoke, born Bashar Barakah Jackson, was an American rapper and songwriter who impacted the music industry during his short career. With his distinctive voice and raw talent, he quickly became one of the brightest stars in hip-hop.

  • 1999: Pop Smoke was born on July 20, in Brooklyn, New York, United States.
  • 2018: He began his music career, releasing his first single, “MPR”, on SoundCloud.
  • 2019: Pop Smoke released his breakout single “Welcome to the Party,” which helped to establish him as one of the most exciting new artists in hip hop. He also released his debut mixtape, “Meet the Woo,” which gained critical acclaim and led to a recording deal with Republic Records.
  • 2020: He released his second mixtape, “Meet the Woo 2,” which debuted at number 7 on the U.S. Billboard 200 chart. He tragically passed away on February 19 following a home invasion robbery in Hollywood Hills, California.
  • 2021: His posthumous album, “Shoot for the Stars, Aim for the Moon,” was released and became a commercial and critical success. It debuted at number one on the U.S. Billboard 200 chart, and several songs from the album gained widespread popularity.
