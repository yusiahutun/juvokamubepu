The Real Housewives of Nairobi cast

The Real Housewives of Nairobi (RHONairobi) is a much-anticipated reality TV show. Fans of the franchise have always wondered what it would be like if the show shot a Kenyan version. The fantasy is over, and now fans can enjoy their rendition of one of the world's most-viewed reality TV franchises. The icing; they'll witness casts, landmarks and locations familiar to them, ones they can easily relate with. So who are the Real Housewives of Nairobi cast?

1. Minne Kariuki

Minne Kariuki is an actress and entrepreneur. She is also a mother and wife. Minne is married to businessman Charles Muigai with whom they have two daughters. She is the youngest of the five housewives and is sassy, bold and unapologetic about who she is.

Minne was born and brought up in Nairobi's Eastlands area. According to an interview on the Cynthia Nyamai Show, Minne revealed that she was born in Nairobi Kariobangi estate before moving to Buruburu. Her father was a subcontractor in the construction industry.

Minne had always wanted to be a lawyer but found herself in the acting industry. She landed her first job as a presenter at KBC, hosting a youth agriculture show. Minne later landed an acting role at Tabasamu, a Citizen TV drama show, and at Ma Empress on Showmax.

After Tabasamu got cancelled, Minne decided to venture into construction. She became an agent at a ballast site in Mlolongo, her first business. However, it could have been smoother sailing initially, as she lost one of her trucks to auctioneers after defaulting on a loan. Minne regained her footing by pushing and became successful through hard work and perseverance.

2. Sonal Maherali

Sonal Maherali is a Kenyan fashion collector, luxury influencer and business lady. She has a luxury blog on YouTube that covers a wide range of stuff, including fashion, shows, travel and lifestyle. In addition, she is a mental health awareness advocate.

4. Lisa Christoffersen

Lisa Christoffersen is the curator of Luxury Safaris, an interior designer, writer, boss, and rally driver. She is in her fifties and has two grown-up children. Lisa founded the first women-only rally team in Kenya, Lioness Rally and Lifestyle Nairobi. She loves cars and is the brand ambassador of Mobius, a registered Kenyan re-assembly company producing vehicles for the African market.

Lisa Christoffersen was born in Moshi, Tanzania. Her father was a wheat farmer, a rally driver and a hunter. Lisa grew up on the slopes west of Mt Kilimanjaro in Tanzania and attended boarding school in Arusha, Tanzania.

When she was 10, her parents' marriage crumbled. Lisa and her mother moved to Denmark, which was their ancestral origin. She also enrolled in school there, spending 12 years learning multiple languages.

Lisa landed her first job at Copenhagen International airport at 18. She came to Kenya when she was 22 at the invitation of her auntie, who had lived there for years. She worked at Safari Park hotel as an interior designer before relocating to Tanzania with her then-husband.

5. Susan Kaittany

Susan Kaittany is a 39-year-old lawyer and entrepreneur. She owns Polished, a luxury fragrance and skincare shop, and Posh Palace Hair Studio and Spa. The mother of two abandoned her legal career to pursue modelling and travel. She has also dabbled in lifestyle and beauty entrepreneurship and is doing well.

But with all the fame and wealth, you would easily confuse Susan as a child of a wealthy family. However, it is the contrary. Susan Kaittany was born in 1984 to a civil servant father and a teacher mother. She grew up in a government house in Langata and attended school at Langata West, where her mother taught.
