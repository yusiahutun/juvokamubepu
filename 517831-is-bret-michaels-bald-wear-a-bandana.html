Who is Bret Michaels?

Bret Michaels is the lead singer of the famous American rock band, Poison. The rock band has sold over 15 million records in the United States of America alone.

On top of being the band's frontman, Bret has numerous solo albums to his credit. One of the most popular is the soundtrack album to the 1998 movie, A Letter from Death Row.

Bret was born in Chicora, Pennsylvania, on March 15, 1963. This means that the talented musician is 63 years old. Bret grew up in Mechanicsburg, Pennsylvania and attended Mechanicsburg Area Senior High School.

Michaels started playing the guitar as a teenager and mastered the craft. He created a band with drummer Rikki Rocket, bass player Bobby Dall and guitarist David Besselman. However, Besselman left the band due to creative differences.

The band changed its name to Poison in early 1984 and moved to West Los Angeles to seek fame on the Sunset Boulevard scene. And it has been an impressive journey ever since, with the band significantly impacting the music scene.
