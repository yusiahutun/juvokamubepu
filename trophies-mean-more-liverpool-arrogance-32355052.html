'Trophies don't mean more at Liverpool their arrogance is off the charts' - Harry Brent

You probably caught Trent Alexander-Arnold’s “trophies mean more to Liverpool” comment last week, and honestly what a load of nauseating tosh.

The idea of a Liverpudlian being both annoying and arrogant isn’t exactly a shock, but have their delusions of grandeur ever been more insufferable? (Yes, obviously. But anyway).

It’s the worst case of main character syndrome since Friends gave Joey that awful spin-off. The Anfield club are owned by an investment group worth £20billion. They’ve broken the world transfer record for both a goalkeeper and a defender (and were a Chelsea hijack away from doing the same for a midfielder last summer).

Sod off Tim Sherwood

Tim Sherwood annoys me on a cellular level. The ex-Tottenham boss is as washed up as a rubber duck in a tsunami yet he still runs his mouth like he’s cock of the walk (emphasis on cock).

The fact he’s still harping on about ‘discovering’ Harry Kane suggests he’ll do anything for attention, and on Monday he took the phrase ‘look at me, I’m rad’ to egotistical new lows while interviewing Cole Palmer.

Like the drunk, racist uncle at a wedding, he cornered the Chelsea ace by inviting him to slag off his team-mates with a cheap line about him being surrounded by mediocrity (I suppose Timmy Boy's an expert on mediocrity). When Palmer refused to bite, Sherwood blurted back: “You can’t say it, but I can.”

We get it, Tim. You’re edgy and you ‘tell it like it is’. But all you really are is a self-absorbed blowhard with as many brain cells as you have wins as a manager. Kindly sod off, will you?

Contactless football sucks

Football is dissolving into a contactless mush and I’m getting sick of it. It’s great that the days of Ben Thatcher only getting booked for elbowing Pedro Mendes into oblivion are over, but the game is losing part of its soul - the rough and tumble.

Seeing Garnacho writhe in agony after getting his shin tapped by Tarkowski at the weekend made my blood boil. Players just wait for the slightest touch before hitting the deck like an angry blackjack dealer, because physical contact is all that’s needed nowadays.

Thankfully, officials saw sense during the Doku-Mac Allister drama at the end of Liverpool vs Man City (fight me, Scousers). Yes, boot met body, but Klopp’s claim that his No. 10 “could have been killed” was as batsh*t as Bruce Wayne’s septic tank.

Remember when John Terry got volleyed in the head in the 2007 League Cup final and everyone just got on with it? Those were the bloody days.
