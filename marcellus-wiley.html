Who is Marcellus Wiley?

Marcellus Vernon Wiley Sr. is a former American Football Defensive End who played 10 seasons in the National Football League for four teams.

He was selected with the 22nd pick of the second round of the 1997 NFL Draft out of Columbia University by the Buffalo B.

After four seasons with the Buffalo Bills, he played for the San Diego Chargers, Dallas Cowboys, and JacksonvilleJaguars.

He was selected to the AFC Pro Bowl team in 2001, as a member of the San Diego Chargers.

He also hosted SportsNation on ESPN as well as afternoon drive-time sports talk radio show in Los Angeles and are currently a member of Fox Sports 1’s “Speak For Yourself”.

In 2018, Wiley’s book Never Shut Up: The Life, Opinions, and Unexpected Adventures of an NFL Outlier were released.

Wiley’s Professional Career

Wiley started his professional career originally as a pass rusher in which he recorded nine sacks through his first three years.

In 2000, Wiley replaced the Hall of Famer Bruce Smith who left for the Washington Redskins at defensive end for the Bills. 

He was voted to the Pro Bowl once in his ten-year career in the year 2001.

In his 10-year career, he played 147 games in which he had 238 tackles, 82 assisted tackles, 44.0 sacks, 13 forced fumbles, and 4 fumble recoveries combining up to 320 tackles.  

Wiley was a cofounder of Prolebrity, a sports community where pro athletes express viewpoints, publicize their businesses, charities, and events, and connect with other athletes, fans, and business opportunities.

Wiley worked for ESPN’s NFL Live and was a substitute co-host for Mike and Mike in The Morning.

He also co-hosted SportsNation and Winners Bracket along with Michelle Beadle from 2010 to 2012. After that, he also hosted “Afternoons on ESPNLA with Marcellus Wiley and Travis Rogers.”

In January 2013, he finally became the co-host of ESPN on a full-time basis but he left ESPN on 13 July 2018 to join Fox Sports 1 as a co-host of “Speak for Yourself”.

In 2018, Wiley’s book “Never Shut Up The Life, Opinions, and Unexpected Adventures of an NFL Outsider” was released.

Wiley was also seen on a November 2011 episode of Bravo’s Millionaire Matchmaker. Season 5 Episode 12: “The Player and The Piano Player” ended when Wiley Successfully found a romantic interest.
