Early life

The NBC host was born in Norman, Oklahoma but grew up in different states and countries. Hoda Kotb's family moved a lot, so she enjoyed various cultures and lifestyles throughout her childhood. Together with her two siblings, they lived in states like Morgantown, West Virginia, and Alexandria.

The family of five also lived temporarily in Nigeria and Egypt. Kotb's mother works at the Library of Congress, while her father worked as a fossil energy specialist until 1986 when he passed away.

Hoda attended Fort Hunt High School in Alexandria, where she graduated as the homecoming queen in 1982. She then proceeded to Virginia Tech to pursue higher education. While there, she joined the Delta Delta Delta sorority and worked at WUVT, the student radio station

Career

Kotb knew from an early age that she wanted to be a journalist. Her first job was at CBS in 1986, where she worked as a news assistant. The 56-year-old worked in her parents' home town, Cairo, for some time in this capacity. She also worked briefly for ABC in Illinois.

The ever-smiling TV host later moved to WQAD-TV to serve as a general assignment reporter. Her next assignment was at WINK-TV Fort Myers, Florida as its weekend anchor and reporter. Then between 1992 and 1998, Kotb was a reporter with WWL-TV in New Orleans, Louisiana.

Hoda then moved to NBC as a national correspondent for Dateline. Her new role became the stepping stone for her fame and global recognition. In 2004, the journalist began hosting a weekly series, Your Total Health. She held this position until 2004. Three years later, Kotb became a co-host for NBC's Today show.

The opportunity coincided with the debut of a new 10 a.m hour show, which she took up in 2008 alongside Kathy Lee Gifford. Kotb served in this position until 2018. She then succeeded Matt Lauer as a co-anchor after accusations of sexual harassment.

Hoda co-anchors the show with Samatha Guthrie, and the two became the first female duo to host Today since its inception. The two women have proven to be a powerhouse, and they increased the show's viewership significantly.

Acting

Apart from her hosting work, Hoda is also a successful actress. Her acting debut was in Lipstick Jungle (2009), a comedic drama on NBC. Kotb also appeared in two episodes of Law & Order: Special Victims Unit in 2014.

Other film appearances by the TV personality include Girlfriends' Guide to Divorce ((2014-2017), Lip Sync Battle (2015) and Sharknando 3: Oh Hell No (2015). Kotb was also in Sharknando 5: Global Swarming (2017) and Mickey and the Roadster Racers (2017).

Unknown to many, the TV queen has also featured in music videos. She was in the video for the song I'm Gonna Love You Through It by Martina McBride.

Marriage

The Today host was married to Burzis Kanga between 2005 and 2008. Burzis was the former tennis coach for the University of New Orleans, but the two went separate ways after only three years. The details of the divorce are not in public.

In 2013, she met Joel Schiffman at a book signing event where she was speaking. Joel is the Vice President and director of Janus Capital Group, a financial institution headquartered in New York.

The two hit it off immediately after she had signed his book. They later exchanged emails and then went on a date, after which a romantic relationship blossomed.

Joel and Hoda got engaged in 2019 and were planning their wedding, which they postponed due to the COVID pandemic. They have two children; namely, Haley Joy, adopted in February 2017, and Hope Catherine, adopted in April 2019.

Surviving cancer

In early 2017, during a routine check-up, Kotb's gynaecologist discovered lumps in her breasts. Later, she was diagnosed with breast cancer. Hoda had never undergone a mammogram before. So as a way of treatment, Kotb opted for mastectomy instead of chemotherapy.

The reconstructive procedure was successful, and she went through a prolonged period of recovery post-surgery. Kotb shared her experience during one of her shows and has since become an ambassador for cancer awareness.
