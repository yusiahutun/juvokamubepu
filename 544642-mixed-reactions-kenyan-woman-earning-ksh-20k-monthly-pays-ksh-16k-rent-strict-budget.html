Grateful Form 4 Student Impresses Netizens With Beautiful Handwriting in Letter to Sponsor

  • A Form Four student has captivated internet users with a meticulously handwritten letter expressing her appreciation to her sponsor
  • The student's letter not only conveyed her gratitude but also showcased her exceptional handwriting skills, leaving a lasting impression on all who read it
  • She eloquently detailed her experiences during the first term of school and expressed her gratitude for her sponsor's unwavering support

A handwritten letter from a Form Four student has managed to capture the hearts of netizens on social media platforms.

Why was the student writing the letter?

The letter, penned with remarkable care and elegance, was a sincere expression of gratitude to her sponsor for the invaluable support she has received.

Addressed to Ndungu Nyoro, the founder of Affecto Foundation, the letter showed the student's appreciation and determination to excel academically.

Shared on Nyoro's Facebook page, the letter provided a glimpse into the student's journey through the first term of school as a Form Four student.

She began by expressing her gratitude for her sponsor's support, acknowledging the role it has played in her academic success.

"I have been fairing well in school, no illnesses have attacked me all throughout the term, thanks to the Almighty," she wrote.

Students promise to perform better in exams

The letter also offered insight into the student's daily life at school, emphasising the dedication of her teachers and her commitment to academic improvement.

With pride, she shared her progress, noting an impressive rise in her grades from a B plus to an A minus.

But perhaps the most compelling aspect of the letter was the student's unwavering determination to succeed.

"Dad, thank you for believing in me," she writes. "I promise to bring glory home by getting an A plain in the KCSE."

Nyoro, upon receiving the letter, expressed his pride in the student's achievements, noting the importance of education in empowering young minds.

Nyandarua student impresses with Kiswahili language

In a related report, a Form One student from Karima Girls High School in Nyandarua impressed Kenyans on social media after her handwritten letter to her well-wisher was shared.

The brilliant student and an apparent aficionado of the Swahili language penned the most beautiful letter to the Affecto Foundation headed by Ndung'u Nyoro, which sponsored her to high school.

In the neatly written note shared by Nyoro, the young girl explained her plans for the April holiday and, with her distinguished flair for the Swahili language, exuded hope that her high school journey would go on seamlessly.
