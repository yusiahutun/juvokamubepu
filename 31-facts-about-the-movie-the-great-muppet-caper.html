A Muppet Adventure in London

Released in 1981, “The Great Muppet Caper” is the second feature film in the Muppets franchise, following the success of “The Muppet Movie”.

Directed by Jim Henson

The film was directed by Jim Henson, the creator of the Muppets, and marked his second directorial effort for a Muppet film.

The Muppets play dual roles

In “The Great Muppet Caper,” the main Muppet characters, including Kermit the Frog, Miss Piggy, and Fozzie Bear, play dual roles as both themselves and as humans.

A Crime Caper in London

The movie is set in London and follows the Muppets as they investigate and solve a jewelry heist, unraveling a comedic adventure along the way.

Cameo appearances

The film features numerous cameo appearances, including John Cleese, Peter Falk, and Jack Warden, adding to the star-studded cast.

Infectious Musical Numbers

“The Great Muppet Caper” is known for its infectious musical numbers, composed by Joe Raposo, including the popular song “The First Time It Happens”.

Muppet antics and humor

The movie is filled with classic Muppet antics and humor, keeping audiences entertained with their unique blend of wit and charm.

The love story between Kermit and Miss Piggy

The film further explores the love-hate relationship between Kermit the Frog and Miss Piggy, adding a romantic subplot to the comedic caper.

Memorable Muppet characters

In addition to Kermit and Miss Piggy, other beloved Muppet characters such as Gonzo, Fozzie Bear, and Animal make appearances, delighting fans of all ages.

Critics’ and audience acclaim

“The Great Muppet Caper” received positive reviews from both critics and audiences, who praised its humor, music, and overall Muppet charm.

Box office success

The movie was a box office success, grossing over $31 million worldwide, solidifying the Muppets’ popularity on the big screen.

Sequel to the original Muppet film
