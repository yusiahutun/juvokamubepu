Mixed Reactions as Kenyan Woman Earning KSh 20k Monthly Says She Pays KSh 16k Rent: &quot;Strict Budget&quot;

  • A young Kenyan woman has sparked a flurry of reactions after she revealed her monthly budgeting and living expenses
  • The woman shared her financial situation in response to a TikToker who was discussing the correlation between net income and rent
  • In her response, @wagitheru disclosed that she earns a net salary of KSh 20,000 but pays a hefty KSh 16,000 for rent

A Kenyan woman has triggered a wave of mixed reactions after disclosing her monthly income and living expenses.

How does the woman use her salary?

Identified on TikTok as @wagitheru, the woman left netizens both intrigued and concerned about her financial situation.

She shared her financial reality in response to a video by fellow TikToker @ivyoyoriofficial, who discussed the relationship between net income and suitable rent payments.

@wagitheru revealed that she earns a net salary of KSh 20,000 but allocates a significant portion, KSh 16,000, towards rent, leaving her with KSh 4,000 to cover all other monthly expenses.

She went on to explain that she occasionally skips meals to stretch her budget, emphasising her preference for residing in a comfortable home despite financial constraints.

'I earn 20k and live in a 16k house. the secret is you bend somedays I don't have to eat daily. I prefer living in a good house," she wrote.

Divorced woman asks new man she's dating to pay rent

In a separate story, a woman who was divorced years ago was glad when she found love again in the hands of another man.

The two have been dating for a while, and the new lover ticks all the boxes despite being younger than the divorcee.

However, the difference is that while the man still rents, the woman's mortgage is entirely paid off.

According to The Mirror, this disparity has become a source of disagreement for the lovers.
