The Wolf Cut Is This Season's Most Trending Haircut

As much as we love a razor-sharp blunt cut, there's something to be said of the opposite: a choppy, layered look that is equal parts edgy and effortless. Maybe that's why the season's most covetable hairstyle, the wolf cut, has been favored among celebrities, including Billie Eilish, Miley Cyrus, and Keke Palmer. It's a haircut that feels fresh yet familiar, giving us the nostalgia that we crave but with a modern twist.

Intrigued? We tapped a slew of experts to learn everything there is to know about this trendy haircut, from how to request it at the salon to pro styling tips and our favorite celeb-inspired looks.

Ahead, check out our complete guide to pulling off a wolf cut, regardless of your hair length, type, and texture.

What Is a Wolf Cut?

One of the signature parts of the wolf cut is its volume and layers — lots of layers.

"The wolf haircut is a mix between a vintage shag and a slight mullet," said Sarah Potempa, celebrity stylist and founder of The Beachwaver Co. "Essentially, it's like taking two different periods (the '70s shag and '80s mullet) and smashing them together into a modern-day look."

According to Matrix artistic director Nick Stenson, choppiness and lack of blend in the layers are key to a wolf cut. Typically, this hairstyle will offer shorter layers at the top of the head to enhance volume and texture with longer layers in the back, suggested Potempa. The results offer an edgy, almost undone, messy, rocker vibe.

Who Is a Wolf Cut for?

If you're after a shaggy, tousled look with a boost of volume, this is the style for you. 

"The wolf haircut is going to look better on someone who needs more volume and body, so most people with fine hair love this look on them, but really, it could be rocked by any hair type if you're looking for more volume for your hair," celebrity stylist Lovette Candice told us.

However, if you don't enjoy hair that moves and has a more "lived-in attitude," this may not be the cut for you, noted Stenson. 

"If you are open to a more tousled hair vibe and looking for a great way to spice up your look, you should try it out," he said. Also, this haircut works on all hair lengths, but Pompeo added that if you have shorter hair, the results will look choppier and more piece-y.

What to Ask for When Getting a Wolf Cut

This gender-neutral haircut can be customized in myriad ways depending on your preferences, says Los Angeles-based stylist Sharon Spellman. "My top tip would be to make sure you're prepared on how exactly you want your haircut done and specify whether you want more or fewer layers and volume," Spellman advised.

Since everyone has their own interpretation of a wolf cut, be sure to bring pictures of the specific style you're going for to your salon appointment. Sharing inspiration and a reference with your stylist will help to avoid any miscommunication.

How to Style a Wolf Cut

Curly and Wavy Hair

When it comes to styling a wolf haircut, you've got options. Still, to minimize styling time, considering your natural hair texture is a smart move. For those with naturally curly or wavy hair, this cut is perfect for emphasizing your hair texture. Stenson says all you'll need is a leave-in conditioner and to let it air-dry for a playful, tousled look. Because these hair types tend to be drier, you may want to apply a spray like the Matrix Miracle Creator Multi-Tasking Treatment Spray ($24) to help control any potential frizz.

Straight and Fine Hair

If you have straight and fine hair, you may need the help of some additional hair products and tools to get that tousled look. 

"If you tend to have flat hair, use a heat tool, like the Beachwaver S1.25 ($149), for a slight bend that will give the hair added texture," suggested Pompeo. After you've created the waves, you can add dry shampoo to add volume and sea salt or texturizing spray to make it look more like beachy waves.

As there are no rules to styling a wolf haircut, you can also give yourself a big, bouncy blowout if that's more your speed. It's all about enjoying yourself and experimenting with new ideas. 

"Styling the wolf cut can be so much fun because you can change up the whole vibe by the way you style it," said Candice.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • Should you get a wolf cut if you have thin hair?

    A wolf cut will add volume and body to hair, which can make it appealing to those with thin strands. However, proceed with caution since creating too many choppy layers can actually make hair appear even less dense.

  • Is a wolf cut good for thick hair?


    Yes, thick hair is great for a wolf cut since the layers help to remove some of hair's weight and lend movement. Just make sure your stylist is strategically adding layers so as not to create too much volume, which can result in a boxy look.

  • What face shape does a wolf cut look best on?

    A wolf cut will look flattering on any face shape. Your stylist should be customizing the cut, in terms of layers and length, to complement your face and features.
