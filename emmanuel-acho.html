Who is Emmanuel Acho?

Emmanuel Chinedom Ako, popularly known as Emmanuel Acho, is a former footballer who played in the National Football League(NFL). Furthermore, Acho is also a sports analyst working for Fox Sports. Previously, he was an analyst for one of ESPN’s popular networks. He has played for popular NFL teams like Cleveland Brown, New York Giants, and Philadelphia Eagles. Furthermore, Acho is also known as the brother of NFL player Sam Acho.

Acho is also the co-host of a famous sports talk show named Speak For Yourself. In this show, he stars in the afternoon along with Marcellus Wiley. Emmanuel, the Fox analyst, is also the producer and host of the Youtube interview series Uncomfortable Conversations with a Black Man. Later, Acho released the book with the same name, produced by Oprah Winfrey, the famous actress. Apart from these, Acho is also engaged in social work. Whenever he visits his parents in his hometown in Nigeria, he helps provide medical services to those in need.

Biography TimeLine

Emmanuel Acho is a former NFL player turned television personality and activist. He was born on November 10, 1990, in Dallas, Texas. After playing football at the University of Texas, Acho played for several teams in the NFL before retiring in 2015. Since then, he has become known for his work as a commentator and host on ESPN and other networks and for his activism around racial justice and equality issues.

  • November 10, 1990: Emmanuel Acho is born in Dallas, Texas.
  • 2008: Acho begins playing football at the University of Texas at Austin.
  • 2012: Acho is drafted by the Cleveland Browns in the sixth round of the NFL Draft.
  • 2013-2014: Acho plays for the Philadelphia Eagles, appearing in 22 games over two seasons.
  • 2015: Acho signs with the New York Giants but is released before the start of the regular season, marking the end of his NFL career.
  • 2016: Acho begins working as a college football analyst for ESPN.
  • 2017: Acho joins FS1 as a co-host on “Speak for Yourself.”
  • 2020: Acho launches a web series called “Uncomfortable Conversations with a Black Man,” in which he talks with white guests about issues of race and racism.
  • 2021: Acho releases a book based on the “Uncomfortable Conversations” series, titled “Uncomfortable Conversations with a Black Boy.”
