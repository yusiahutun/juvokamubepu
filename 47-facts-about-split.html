Split has a rich history that dates back over 1,700 years.

Split, located on the stunning Dalmatian Coast of Croatia, was originally founded as a Greek colony in the 3rd century B.C. It later became a significant Roman city.

The Old Town of Split is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

The historic center of Split, known as Diocletian’s Palace, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. This ancient palace complex is one of the best-preserved Roman ruins in the world.

Split is home to the Bell Tower of St. Domnius, a symbol of the city.

The Bell Tower of St. Domnius, built in the 13th century, offers breathtaking panoramic views of Split. It stands as a prominent landmark and a reminder of the city’s medieval past.

Split is the birthplace of the renowned Roman Emperor Diocletian.

Emperor Diocletian, known for his significant reforms in the Roman Empire, was born in Salona, a nearby town to Split. Diocletian’s Palace in Split was built as his retirement residence.

Game of Thrones fans will recognize Split as a filming location.

Several iconic scenes from the popular TV series Game of Thrones were filmed in Split, including the scenes set in the underground cellars of Diocletian’s Palace.

Split is a vibrant cultural hub with numerous art galleries and museums.

Art enthusiasts can explore the impressive collections at the Split City Museum, Museum of Croatian Archaeological Monuments, and the Ivan Meštrovi? Gallery, which showcases the works of the famous Croatian sculptor.

Split is known for its lively waterfront promenade, the Riva.

The Riva, a beloved gathering spot for locals and tourists alike, offers stunning views of the Adriatic Sea. It is lined with cafes, restaurants, and charming shops.

Split has a bustling food scene with delicious local cuisine.

Indulge in traditional Dalmatian dishes such as seafood specialties, peka (slow-cooked meat and vegetables), and pašticada (a hearty beef stew). Don’t forget to try the local wine and rakija (fruit brandy).

Split hosts one of the biggest street parties in Croatia, the Ultra Music Festival.

Every summer, music lovers from around the world gather in Split for the renowned Ultra Music Festival. The festival features electronic music acts and attracts thousands of attendees.

Split is a gateway to the stunning Croatian islands.

From Split, you can easily reach popular nearby islands such as Hvar, Bra?, and Vis. Ferries and speedboats provide convenient transportation options for island hopping.

Split offers a wide range of outdoor activities.

Adventure seekers can go hiking in the nearby Marjan Forest Park, enjoy thrilling water sports along the coast, or explore the natural beauty of Krka National Park.

Split has a Mediterranean climate with long, hot summers and mild, rainy winters.

The pleasant climate makes Split an ideal destination for sun-seekers and outdoor enthusiasts. Summers are perfect for swimming and soaking up the sun on the picturesque beaches.

Split is a popular cruise port in the Adriatic Sea.

Thanks to its strategic location and picturesque charm, Split attracts numerous cruise ships. Visitors arriving by cruise can easily explore the city’s main attractions within walking distance of the port.

Split has a lively nightlife scene.

From trendy cocktail bars to vibrant nightclubs, Split offers a diverse range of options for night owls. The city comes alive after dark, especially during the summer months.

Split has been a cultural crossroads throughout history.

Located at the crossroads of Central Europe and the Mediterranean, Split has been influenced by various cultures, including Roman, Byzantine, Venetian, and Ottoman. This diverse heritage is reflected in its architecture, cuisine, and traditions.

The famous Croatian inventor Nikola Tesla attended school in Split.

Nikola Tesla, known for his contributions to modern electricity, spent part of his childhood in Split, where he attended the Higher Real Gymnasium.

Split is home to the Croatian National Theatre.

The Croatian National Theatre in Split showcases a diverse program of opera, ballet, and dramatic performances. The theater’s beautiful interior adds to the overall cultural experience.

Split is a popular filming location for various international movies.

Aside from Game of Thrones, Split has provided the backdrop for films such as Winnetou and The Twilight Saga: Breaking Dawn.

Split celebrates numerous annual festivals.

The Split Summer Festival, held from mid-July to mid-August, features a variety of cultural events, including theater performances, concerts, and art exhibitions.

The breathtaking Bacvice Beach is located in Split.

Bacvice Beach, known for its soft sandy shores and crystal-clear waters, is a favorite spot for locals and tourists to relax and enjoy the sun.

Split has a vibrant local market, the Green Market.

The Green Market, located near the Diocletian’s Palace, offers a wide range of fresh produce, local delicacies, and handmade crafts. It’s the perfect place to immerse yourself in the local culture.

Split is a haven for history buffs.

The city is dotted with ancient ruins, museums, and historical landmarks that tell the story of Split’s rich past. Exploring its fascinating history is like stepping back in time.

Split is the second-largest city in Croatia.

With a population of approximately 180,000 people, Split is the economic and cultural center of the region.

The Split International Airport connects the city to major European destinations.

Travelers can easily reach Split by air, with frequent flights from major European cities. The airport is conveniently located just a short drive from the city center.

Split is a paradise for architecture enthusiasts.

From ancient Roman structures to intricate Gothic and Renaissance buildings, Split showcases a harmonious blend of architectural styles throughout its streets and squares.

Split hosted the 2018 Mediterranean Games.

The Mediterranean Games, a multi-sport event held every four years, took place in Split, showcasing the city’s ability to host international sporting events.

Split is a gateway to the breathtaking Plitvice Lakes National Park.

Plitvice Lakes National Park, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is located just a few hours’ drive from Split. Its cascading waterfalls and turquoise lakes attract visitors from around the world.

FAQs

1. What is the best time to visit Split?

The best time to visit Split is during the spring (April to June) and fall (September to October) when the weather is pleasant, and tourist crowds are relatively smaller.

2. How do I get to Split?

Split has an international airport, which makes it easily accessible by air. Additionally, you can reach Split by ferry from various nearby islands or by bus from other cities in Croatia.

3. Can I explore Split on foot?

Absolutely! Split’s city center is compact and pedestrian-friendly, making it ideal for exploring on foot. Many of the main attractions, restaurants, and shops are within walking distance of each other.

4. Are there any beaches in Split?

Yes, there are several beautiful beaches in and around Split. Some popular ones include Ba?vice Beach, Kasjuni Beach, and Žnjan Beach, offering a mix of pebbles and sand.

5. Is it safe to visit Split?

Yes, Split is generally a safe city for visitors. However, like any popular tourist destination, it’s always recommended to take standard precautions such as keeping an eye on your belongings and being aware of your surroundings.

6. What are some must-visit attractions in Split?

Some must-visit attractions in Split include Diocletian’s Palace, the Cathedral of Saint Domnius, Marjan Hill, and the Riva promenade. These landmarks offer a glimpse into the city’s rich history and stunning architecture.
