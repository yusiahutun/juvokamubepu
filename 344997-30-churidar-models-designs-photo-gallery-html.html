What is churidar style?

Churidar is a dress style adopted from Indian culture. If you are familiar with Indian fashion, you will notice the resemblance with the typical salwar pants. Both men and women wear this attire.

Women wear it with a kameez (tunic) while men adorn it with a kurta (loose overshirt). The style has been part of Indian culture for centuries, and it is now widely adopted in different nations all over the globe.

What is the difference between salwar and churidar?

If you are not familiar with the origin and adaptation of certain Indian styles in today’s modern culture, you will find a hard time differentiating specific clothing. A non-fashionista might not know the difference between salwar and churidar, and there is a reason why.

The most straightforward distinction between the two is design. However, it is still hard knowing which is which. The salwar is a traditional version, while churidar is a modern variant. Churidars are designed to be more fitting from the waist to the ankles. Salwars are not.

What are the types of churidar?

If you look at the latest churidar models, you will notice that there are specific designs that most mimic. These are the distinctive classes, groups or types that most designers create. Since they are salwar variants, they are classified according to different salwar designs.

There are many types, but most categorize them into a minimum of six. You will find Patiala suits, straight suits, salwar suits, palazzo suits and many more.

What is churidar called in English?

The dressing style is so typical that it has made its way into the English dictionary. In British English, it still maintains the name and does not have a translation. The dictionary defines the term as a long fitting trouser worn by Indian men and women.

Numerous people start looking for churidar models and designs when they are going to formal occasions. However, the dressing style has experienced massive adoption into different cultures that it is fast becoming versatile clothing, perfect for any event. Initially, you could only find churidars in plan colours, but now, there are many designs.
