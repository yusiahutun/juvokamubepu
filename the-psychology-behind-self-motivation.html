The psychology behind self-motivation

People have lots of dreams, plans, and ambitions in their lives. They often feel that they can achieve what they desire if they find the right direction and resources. But most of the time, people do not get or achieve what they want in life. Have you ever thought why, even when they have the resources and capacity to work, they cannot complete their ambition? The simple answer to this question most of the time is that they lack self-motivation. People indulge in their day-to-day challenges and forget to work on what they want in their lives. Let’s talk about self-motivation in detail.

What is self-motivation?

Self-motivation is an inner force that pushes the person to constantly work in the direction of their goal, dream, ambition or vision. This inner force comes from their passion, interest, and values. A self-motivated person feels that they want to improve themselves in terms of performance and skills. They do not require any other person to remind them of their task to be conducted.

A self-motivated person works even when they experience setbacks in their life. They take up the opportunity and show commitment to their work even when situations do not go according to plan. In other words, they have a good level of resilience to handle challenges and failures in life. A self-motivated person can control their emotions and aspects of life better than others.

Elements of motivation:

1) Personal drive to achieve:

People who have an appropriate level of inner drive to achieve their dreams are good at avoiding distraction, organizing their tasks in coordination with their plans, and making maximum use of their time. They believe in utilizing every opportunity they encounter. This drive for improvement leads to a growth mindset. They believe in possibilities and their ability to improve their skills through effort and hard work.
Research indicates that people with a growth mindset have better chances of achieving their aspirations than people who do not work on their own skills.

2) Commitment towards Goals:

Humans are filled with emotional energy, and when this energy gets channeled in the right direction, they feel happy as well as lead a worthy life. A self-motivated person’s actions and thoughts are in sync with their goal, as they have dedication and commitment towards their dream. This leads them to have a better focus on optimizing their performance. Commitment helps a self-motivated person go on in life even when they don’t want to due to their limitations. An individual needs to have a purpose in their life that creates meaning for their actions.

3) Initiative:

It’s really important to have good initiative skills when a person wants to achieve something in their life, as actions taken at the right time provide more worth to the action and generate more possibilities. Initiative skills are about being ready to take charge of situations or take advantage of opportunities when they occur. It’s a combination of courage, confidence, and acceptance of challenges.
It’s easy for a person to hesitate, but it’s really hard to digest the outcome of its consequences. To be good at initiative skills, a person has to overcome the idea of being negatively judged by others.

4) Optimism or resilience:

it’s obvious to fall while walking, but how can people fall when they are in their comfort zone? For anyone who has a purpose or aim in their life, they have to accept the fact that they will fail in life; things will not work out their way, but they still have to cross the hurdles as they did in childhood while playing games. Resilience is the ability to bounce back after setbacks or keep moving in the right direction regardless of severe challenges. Optimism is about being hopeful and believing in the bright side of any situation. Resilience People can manage negative emotions. They ask for help when it is required, but they are also self-reliant.

Are intrinsic motivation and self-motivation synonyms?

These two concepts are largely connected and share the same energy platform (passion, the internet, and values). But these two words are not identical, as in a broader sense, they have some differences as well. For example, a person who is intrinsically motivated will work for their inner satisfaction without any external support or reward. Whereas in self-motivation, a person can use external rewards to enhance their motivation level, such as if they complete their task successfully, they will go for coffee with friends, a movie, or quality time with family. In these situations, external rewards facilitate their performance.

Relationship between self-motivation and self-efficacy

Self-efficacy is about an individual’s belief in their potential to execute behavior to meet their goal. Once people believe in themselves and their abilities, they can only be motivated or directed to perform their actions to attain the goal of their lives. In other words, self-efficacy is also about calculating the severity of the challenge in comparison to the person’s potential, and a good level of self-efficacy helps the person stay motivated as they perceive their actions as bigger than their challenges.

5 simple Rules of Self-motivation

  • When people remind themselves of their goals in life, they are less likely to be distracted from their action plans.
  • It’s important to utilize the time. Time is limited, but we take it as it’s infinite. Sometimes action becomes meaningless once the right time is over. So it’s necessary to use time while people are in the process of achieving their dreams.
  • Everyone has a certain weakness, and it’s okay to have it. But when it comes to your aim or goal in life, an individual needs to learn to overcome their limitations or seek help from others. Keep believing in the process of rethinking and reassessing their potential for better results.
  • Distractions can make the person feel tired or incapable of meeting objectives. Minimalism and distraction can help a person focus more on their task. To do so, they first have to figure out what their potential distraction is and how it can be avoided.
  • Strive for possibilities that are too positive. Think, act, and reflect, and then start again. Keep this process in action until you do not achieve what you desire.
    • Benefits of being self-motivated:
  • Self-motivation helps the person be engaged and productive in their life.
  • It helps the person cope with life’s challenges and setbacks without any external support.
  • They are their own support system.
  • They have better life satisfaction than others.
  • They have fewer expectations from others, hence having better relationships.
  • Their well-being improves as they focus on their lives.
  • They can accept feedback or criticism of their work and take effective action.
    • Tips for improving self-motivation
  • Self-reflection is necessary for self-motivation. It’s to spare some time for yourself to know who you are, what you want, and what you can do to achieve your wants. In other words, self-evaluation will help to enhance your motivation level.
  • Flexible thinking is the foundation of self-motivation. Don’t try to fit yourself into the character of a victim or hero. Just act and learn from your experience.
  • Practice a growth mindset and learn to work on yourself.
  • A positive attitude towards challenges Is ready to accept challenges, as they have hidden opportunities.
  • Develop a healthy habit that makes you feel fit and healthy.
  • Take charge of your life by making decisions as per your choice. Avoid being dependent on others’ opinions.
  • Have a clear vision for your goal. It should be measurable, specific, achievable, relevant, and time-bound.
  • Even when you are not feeling well, just remain in the flow of work. Gradually, your negative feelings will disappear.
  • Stay connected to your surroundings to know what is happening in the world and the area in which you want to excel.
  • Work forward, and gradually you will find people with similar interests.
