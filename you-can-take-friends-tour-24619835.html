You can take the F.R.I.E.N.D.S. tour of London with these must-see landmarks

Tourists at New York often flock to a Manhattan apartment block to take photos. For the unassuming, it doesn't like much, nothing to compare the Statue of LIberty.

But TV fans this block is a must-visit landmark for fans of the American comedy Friends.

However, fans of the iconic sitcom do not have to travel all the way to New York to visit Friends landmarks. Here in London, there are plenty of fun places to visit from the time the cast visited the beautiful city.

In the final episodes of season four, the Friends cast fly to London, baby - except Phoebe, who was heavily pregnant with Frank Jr and Alice’s triplets.

By heading to different landmarks in the city, fans can have their very own Friends tour of London. To help, 2Chill has created a list of places the cast visited to make sure fans to find themselves lost Joey.

London Marriott Hotel, Grosvenor Square

In the episode, viewers see Joey and Chandler leave the London Marriott Hotel in Grosvenor Square to the tune of London Calling by The Clash.

The characters were staying at the hotel for the duration of the trip, with Joey filming his own little video tape - what an OG travel vlogger.

It’s here we see Joey pull out his map, place it on the ground and say: “I’m going to have to go into the map.”

If you wanted to make your Friends tour even more realistic, you could book a room for the night - which could cost between £273 and £729.

Westminster Abbey

When Joey ‘stepped into’ the map, he was trying to find Westminster Abbey, so you’ll want to head there next.

“Alright! Westminster Abbey! Hands down, best Abbey I've ever seen!” he tells Phoebe on his return to the Big Apple.

The Abbey, one of the UKs most important and iconic buildings, has stood since 1066, and costs £18 per person to enter.

On your way out, check out Big Ben and The Houses of Parliament too.

Trinity Place, Poplar

You may not find Sir Richard Branson selling Union Jack hats, but you can take a gander at the stalls lining the Thames to pick up your own London souvenir.

In the episode, Richard, making a cameo appearance, said: “So, what are you fine gentlemen in the market for? Scarves? Souvenir postcards?”

Joey picks up the novelty hat and tries it on, asking Chandler what he thinks, to which he responds: “Well, I’m not going to have to buy that I’m with stupid t-shirt anymore.”

Tower Bridge

Only briefly appearing in the episode is Tower Bridge, whilst Chandler and Joey ride the open-top bus.

Buy a ticket to the hop-on hop-off tour from City Sightseeing for £29, and it’ll be one of the easiest ways to see London’s iconic landmarks in the same way Joey did, seeing as Chandler fell asleep…

Built between 1886 and 1894, you can also do a tour of Tower Bridge for just £10.60.

Buckingham Palace

Buckingham Palace is fleetingly seen on some of the b-roll that Joey filmed on his camcorder. This is because allegedly, the Queen didn’t want the palace to be in the background of any scenes!

In the episode, Joey bumps into Sarah Ferguson, who films a message to Chandler saying: "Hi Chandler, Joey says you don’t really like his hat, but I think it’s kind of dashing.”

The mother of Princess Eugenie and Beatrice had divorced Prince Andrew at this point, but reportedly the Queen disapproved of her cameo.

Because they couldn’t film outside Buckingham Palace, this scene was shot in front of the Houses of Parliament and Big Ben instead.

St. John's Church, Wapping

It was on Wapping High Street that Monica, Ross and Emily discover that the wedding venue is, in fact, a building site as the church is being demolished.

And whilst the wedding scenes were likely filmed at The Fountain Studios, in Wembley, the exterior of the venue was the stunning St John’s Church.
