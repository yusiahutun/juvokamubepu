From swapping Kronenbourg for Carlsberg and gin for vodka - the easy ways to cut the calories in you

MORE than half of us want to see calorie counts on our booze, according to a YouGov survey.

Weight-conscious drinkers say they find it hard to know how much they are sipping because few manufacturers add this information to the labels of beers, wines and spirits.

Alcohol contains seven calories per gram, almost as much as a gram of fat which contains nine calories.

GP and weight-loss expert Dr Aishah Iqbal warns booze can play havoc with your diet.

She says: “When you consume alcohol, your body considers it a toxin and prioritises its breakdown.

“The metabolism of fat is temporarily halted until the alcohol is processed.
