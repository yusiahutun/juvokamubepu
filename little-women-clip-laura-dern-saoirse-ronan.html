Watch Laura Dern &amp; Saoirse Ronan in a Sweet 'Little Women' Scene

  • An exclusive new clip from Little Women shows Marmee March and Jo March having a heartfelt mother-daughter discussion about dealing with passion and emotions.
  • Marmee says a real-life quote by author Louisa May Alcott's mother: "There are some natures too noble to curb and too lofty to bend."
  • The film premieres on December 25, 2019.

In a new clip from Greta Gerwig's Little Women—premiering here on BAZAAR.com—Marmee March (Laura Dern) admits what most of us are feeling in 2019. "I'm angry nearly every day of my life," she sighs. In this tender, quiet scene, her daughter Jo March (Saoirse Ronan) is just as surprised as we are. "You are?" she whispers back. After all, this is the loving, levelheaded March matriarch, not Renata Klein growling "I will not not be rich!" through gritted teeth.

Huddled on the floor with their backs against the bed, the duo explore different ways of coping with anger. Jo says that she gets "so savage, I could hurt anyone—and I enjoy it." Meanwhile, Marmee, through years of practice, has learned not to let her emotions get the better of her. When Jo says she wants to follow in her footsteps, Marmee answers with some advice: "I hope you'll do a great deal better than me. There are some natures too noble to curb and too lofty to bend."

That last line is enough to induce chills, but the true story behind it makes it even better. Apparently, Dern found the quote in a letter while doing her research for the role, and then collaborated with Gerwig to work it into the script. It was penned by Louisa May Alcott's mother, Abigail, while referring to the bold and spirited Little Women author. "I believe there are some natures too noble to curb, too lofty to bend. Of such is my Lu," she wrote. Consider these words to live by for caring mothers who don't want their daughters to compromise who they are at heart.

When Dern and Ronan aren't having a heart-to-heart onscreen, they're joined by a stellar cast that includes Emma Watson as Meg March, Florence Pugh as Amy March, Eliza Scanlen as Beth March, Timotheé Chalamet as Laurie, and Meryl Streep as Aunt March. Gerwig's long-awaited Little Women adaptation opens in theaters on Christmas Day.
