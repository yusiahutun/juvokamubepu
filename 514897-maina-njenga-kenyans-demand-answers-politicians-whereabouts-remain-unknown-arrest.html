&quot;Where Is Maina Njenga? Kenyans Demand Answers as Politician's Whereabouts Remain Unknown after Arre

  • Former Mungiki sect leader Maina Njenga was allegedly arrested in his father's house in Ngong, Kajiado county
  • Justice Diana Kavedza Mochache ordered the police to produce and free Njenga and not to arrest him unless he fails to do as directed
  • Most Kenyans online called on the government to disclose his present location at least to his immediate family members

Nairobi - Former Mungiki leader Maina Njenga was arrested allegedly over his role in the three-day anti-government demonstration.

Njenga was allegedly picked up by police from his father's house in Ngong at 8 pm on Wednesday, July 19.

The police, allegedly using unmarked cars, informed the family that they wanted to question the former leader of the outlawed sect on the ongoing protests.

His lawyer, Ndegwa Njiru, confirmed the arrest, saying they are yet to establish the police station in which his client is being held.

Court orders Njenga's release.

On Friday, July 21, Justice Diana Kavedza ruled that Njenga's lawyers' request for his immediate and unconditional presentation before the court or the court to order his release was urgent.

She ordered Njenga to be released after payment of KSh 100,000 bail, and he would report back to the DCI and the DPP on Monday, July 24, upon which he should be presented in court.

Justice Kavedza said Njenga should not be arrested unless he failed to show up at the DCI and DPP offices on the aforementioned dates.

She set for the case to be mentioned on Tuesday, September 26.

However, Kenyans have taken to social media platforms asking to know the whereabouts of the controversial politician.

Most questioned why the police were yet to disclose his location despite the court issuing orders for his release.

Njenga's son files suit demanding police to produce him

Earlier, Njenga's son Stephen Maina filed a habeas corpus application to have the police compelled to produce him.

According to Stephen, his father was abducted by unknown people he suspects to be police officers because they were using government vehicles.

"Maina Stephen Njenga the undersigned herein, being the eldest son to Maina Njenga Kamunya, hereby authorises the firm of Ndisi Munje and Associates Advocates to file the relevant proceedings to safeguard the interests of Maina Njenga Kamunya. Peter Njoroge Kamunya and Felix Lekishe," read court papers seen by TUKO.co.ke
