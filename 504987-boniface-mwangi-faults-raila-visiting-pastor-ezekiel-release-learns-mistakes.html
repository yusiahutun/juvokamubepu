Boniface Mwangi Faults Raila for Visiting Pastor Ezekiel After His Release: &quot;Never Learns from Past

  • Raila Odinga visited Pastor Ezekiel Odero at his New Life Prayer Center, making him the first national leader to meet the preacher after his release
  • Boniface Mwangi expressed his annoyance at the ODM Party leader for visiting the embattled preacher
  • The activist said the opposition leader loves making self-defeating actions and does not learn from his mistakes

Human rights activist Boniface Mwangi has blamed Azimio la Umoja One Kenya Alliance leader Raila Odinga for visiting Pastor Ezekiel Odero of New Life Church after the preacher's release.

During the visit, Raila who was accompanied by pastor Ezekiel's lawyers among them Cliff Ombeta and Kilifi governor Gideon Mung'aro was taken on a tour of the expansive church.

Prosecutors accuse pastor Ezekiel of links to cult leader Paul Mackenzie, who is in custody facing terrorism charges over the deaths of more than 100 people and many of them children in the Shakahola area within Malindi in Kilifi county.

He said the ODM Party leader loves scoring own goals and doesn't learn from his past mistakes.

"Raila Odinga loves scoring own goals! Life is brutal to those who never learn from their past mistakes," Mwangi wrote.

Mwangi accuses police of arresting innocent people

On Monday, March 20, Mwangi expressed displeasure at how the police were handling the anti-government protests across the country.

Mwangi complained about the fact that security officers were clearly arresting innocent people who were not part of the demonstrations.

In a video shared on his Facebook page, a contingent of police officers was seen surrounding a boda boda rider who was carrying a pillion along Argwings Kodhek road.
