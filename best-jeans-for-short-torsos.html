The 14 Best Jeans for Short Torsos in 2024

Finding a good pair of jeans when you have a short torso is like winning the lottery. Read: It can feel nearly impossible. As someone with a short torso I know this to be true. For as long as I can remember, I’ve searched for denim that’ll make me appear longer, leaner, and taller in all the right places. But, time and time again, I’ve ended up with jeans that drown my figure and make my already-short torso look even shorter — until now.

In order to find jeans that fit (and flatter) women with short torsos, we took it upon ourselves to uncover the best pairs available. To do so, we interviewed fashion experts to better understand what makes denim suitable for these figures. “The goal is to add length where possible,” explains Zadrian Smith of the styling duo Zadrian + Sarah. “When applying this to jeans, high-waisted options create a lengthening illusion. A flare is also a good pick as it adds lengths and balances your silhouette.”

Complete with expert-recommended brands and styles, this curated list includes the top denim picks that work to elongate legs, compliment curves, and suit short torsos. 

Best Overall

Veronica Beard Beverly High Rise Skinny Flare

Veronica Beard

What We Love: These comfortable jeans are made with a stretchy material that conforms to each body’s unique shape, ultimately accentuating curves.

What We Don’t Love: They can stretch so much that it’s best to size down. 

These top-rated Veronica Beard jeans feature subtle details that elongate short torsos. For starters, the flattering skinny-flare cut instantly lengthens legs by drawing attention downwards, which creates the illusion of more height. Made of a stretchy cotton-elastane blend, the silhouette beautifully hugs your body, keeping you comfortable while simultaneously accentuating curves and defining your waist. The high rise cut lays perfectly along the waistline, cinching it and making your midsection look longer, too. 

We love the timeless blue wash that’s easy to pair with anything, and we appreciate the jeans' versatility: They’re elegant enough to wear to the office when paired with heels, but also feel cool and casual with a pair of combat boots and a leather jacket. The stretch is one of the key selling points of these, but we do recommend sizing down — this way the jeans will form to your body and will be less likely to lose shape over time. 

Size Range: 23W—32W | Rise: High | Material: Cotton, elastomultiester, elastane

What to Keep in Mind

Rise

There are four main types of pant rises: Low , mid, high, and ultra-high rise. In terms of flattering short torsos, the rise you pick ultimately depends on your body shape and the features you want to accentuate. “Each body is so unique – someone may have a short torso and slimmer legs while another might have a short torso and wider legs – both of which are beautiful,” explains Sheth. “How each person feels about their body varies, and the goal is to find a fit that makes you feel the most confident and hits you in the areas that you feel best about.” 

So, if you have a short torso and want to highlight your stomach, we recommend wearing low-rise jeans, like H&M Curvy Fit 90s Baggy Low Jeans. On the other hand, if you have a short torso and want to highlight your legs, wear high- or ultra-high rise jeans, which will extend both your waistline and legs. Along with a high rise fit, Good American Good Curve Straight Jeans features body contouring fabric to hug and accentuate your curves.

Wash

Denim is offered in a range of different washes. There are light, medium, and dark washes as well as black and white. Depending on the brand, you might be able to shop for a pair of jeans in a multitude of washes (such as the Levi’s 501 Original Cropped Women’s Jeans, which offers eight options). However, some specific jean styles might only be offered in one wash. 

Fabric Content

When looking for jeans, consider the fabric they’re made of. Most are made with a mix of cotton, elastane, spandex, and polyester. 

“One hundred percent cotton is stiff and the fibers themselves will not stretch, which has its benefits,” explains professional stylist and image coach Cortney St Clair. One of the main benefits of stiff cotton jeans (such as the Nudie Jeans Rowdy Ruth) is that they will hold their shape over time, even after multiple washes. However, because this material is stiff, there’s less of a chance the jeans will move with you and conform to your curves. 

“Anything with one to three percent elastane or spandex will give you a nice hug and move with your body,” says St Clair, adding that they will also retain their shape. However, it’s best to avoid anything with more than three percent elastane or spandex as these pants will stretch out as you wear them.

Polyester blends are usually made with a mix of cotton or elastane. What you’ll notice with these kinds of jeans is that they offer more stretch, however, they are still able to retain their shape over time. 

Your Questions, Answered

What types of jeans are best for short torsos?

If you are looking to lengthen the appearance of a short torso, it’s best to opt for high rise jeans. “We like high waisted jeans for short torsos as it extends the waistline,” says co-founders of Grey Bandit, Courtney and Lindsey Glasser. “That being said, it’s all about what you like best.” Ultimately, high rise jeans will create the illusion of a longer torso and legs. Because both of these areas will appear to be stretched, they will make the body look more proportionate overall. 

Additionally, Smith notes that flare jeans are a great option when it comes to enhancing the torso. This is because flare styles work to create balance.

Are high waisted jeans good for short torsos?

Yes, high waisted jeans are flattering for short torsos. This is because they make the waistline and legs look longer. If you want to create even proportions, pair a long shirt with high waisted jeans. “This will pull the eye down over the torso and create a second focal point on the lower half of the body,” explains St Clair.

What shouldn't I wear with jeans for short torsos?

In terms of what not to wear with jeans if you have a short torso, it’s all about creating balance. “Be careful of cutting yourself off too abruptly,” say the Glasser sisters. “Certain belts or tops could cut off your lines if you have a shorter torso.” Additionally, steer clear of crop tops when wearing high-waisted jeans. “They put a horizontal line across the midsection, which will create the illusion of a shorter and wider torso.” explains St Clair. 

How do I find the right jean fit while shopping online?

There’s no doubt about it: Shopping online for jeans is easier said than done. However, there are a few tricks to finding the right pants without trying them on. “Stick to the style that you know fits best,” says fashion designer Libbie Mugrabi. “Also, know your inseam for the length of the pants."

Also, be sure to check out the size guide and read customer reviews. If you’re still at a loss for what size to get, reach out to customer service. “Shopping for denim online can be tricky, but there are experts at each brand who are ready to help you,” note the Glasser sisters.
