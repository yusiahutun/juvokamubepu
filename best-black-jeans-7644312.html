The 16 Best Black Jeans of 2024

If you don’t already own a pair of black jeans, we have to ask: what’s the hold-up? They’re sleek, versatile, and elegant. Plus, they can be dressed up much easier than their indigo counterparts, while also suiting a casual occasion equally as well. Basically, if you’re in the midst of building your capsule wardrobe, then a pair of black denim is a must. But just like blue jeans or even white jeans, for that matter, finding the perfect pair can be tough. That's why we consulted experts and tried a bunch of pairs ourselves to find the absolute best pairs you should buy right now, from distressed picks to ones that perfectly hug your curves. As soon as you have a pair in your rotation, you'll question why it took you so long, too.

Best Overall

Agolde Riley Long High Rise Straight

Agolde

What We Love: This straight jean hugs the body but still has a relaxed feel. 

What We Don’t Love: The brand recommends sizing up if you’re in between sizes. 

Known for body-flattering silhouettes, vintage-inspired designs, and use of high-quality material, Agolde takes the cake when it comes to denim. The Riley Long High Rise Straight style meets all the standards we look for in a good pair of black jeans: They’re ultra-comfortable, stylish, and have a rich color. 

The sleek silhouette and finished hem give these jeans a polished look, and we love that they have a vintage feel to them. We find that a high-rise and straight cut helps to elongate your legs, making us look taller. Plus, this pair also features four utilitarian pockets that are deep enough to store your phone, keys and maybe your wallet. Wear them on casual days with sneakers or to the office with heels or boots. 

Note that the inseam is cropped at 26 inches, which may be a little too short for our tall ladies. The brand also recommends sizing up if you’re in between sizes, as the non-stretch denim can feel a bit tight. 

Size Range: 22—34｜Rise: High｜Materials: Organic cotton, viscose, elastane

Best Budget

Target

What We Love: There is a wide size range, including options for petite, tall, and plus size.

What We Don’t Love: The cropped hem makes this a more of a spring to fall jean, rather than a winter jean.

Don’t sleep on Target’s clothing line Universal Thread. The affordable retailer doesn’t skimp out on style, as proven by these high-rise slim straight jeans. You could wear the cropped look to brunch and to work, and no one would be any wiser that you spent a mere $20 on them. Aside from having a wide size range, the jeans come in a variety of lengths as well. Choose between a short (24-inch inseam), a regular (26-inch inseam), and a tall (28-inch inseam). There are plus-size options as well, marked as “w” for wide, which will curve around your hips comfortably. The cropped nature of the jeans might make it a little difficult to wear in the winter (unless you pair it with ankle boots), but for a budget piece, the pants have quite a lot to offer.

What to Keep in Mind

Style 

With so many different jean styles on the market, it can be hard determining which style is best suited for any given person. Sasha Izum, a personal stylist based in San Diego, emphasizes the need to understand your own physique when picking which style best suits you. “As a stylist, I recommend choosing jeans according to your body type in order to emphasize your strong features with the correct fit,” Izum says. According to Sharp, “High waisted jeans (like the Re/Done High Rise Skinny Boot) are great for women with longer torsos as they wont cut up a woman’s proportions and help elongate [the legs].” Conversely, “I always suggest a mid-rise crop to clients who have smaller torsos and are shorter (typically under 5'4),” she adds. Think Rag & Bone Peyton Mid-Rise Boot if you fit in this category. As an easy guide, Sharp finds that wide-leg denim is typically universally flattering, as well as very trendy right now. Curvy women will love the fit of the Madewell The Curvy Perfect Vintage Straight Jean, as it hugs the waist before extending around the bum, accentuating your hourglass figure.

Fabric 

Most black jeans are constructed from cotton fibers or some blend of fabrics like spandex, polyester, elastane, or viscose. Those that are made of 100% cotton, typically stretch less, but last longer. They often soften up over time, even if they feel a little stiff at first, molding into your perfect fit. Jeans that are made of a blend with elastane feel more comfortable around the body, giving you some breathing room. We like to think that skinny jeans, like the Frame Le High Skinny, should have that stretchy material blended in, while straight or baggy pants, like the E.L.V. Denim Sustainable Black Match Boyfriend Jeans, can get away with more rigid denim. Overall, splurging on jeans that are made with higher-quality cotton will ensure that your jeans keep their shape over time. Oftentimes, cheaper cotton will get loose and lose its color over time. Stylist Sasha Izum says, “Even if you invest just $100-$200 in a pair of jeans, you will end up wearing them forever as they won't lose shape”

Your Questions Answered

What can I wear with black jeans? 

Black jeans can go with numerous different looks and can be taken from day to night with a quick change of shoes or an additional accessory. For stylist Joanne Kennedy, “Black jeans are so versatile, so it is totally dependent on the event, the venue, and the crowd you are with.” For an evening event, Kennedy says “I would select a slimmer, rigid, dark black jeans (as the dark colors tend to be smarter looking), paired with a form-fitting bodysuit and glitzy statement evening bag and killer heels - understated but still impactful.” Stylist Jordanna Sharp is also a fan of styling black denim and recommends “keeping it simple with a gray cardigan and tank top for an everyday look. Sometimes, all black can look a little heavy, so it’s nice to lighten the style up.” 

How do I keep black jeans from fading? 

In general, buying higher quality denim will prevent your jeans from fading, as the material tends to keep its color and shape better. That being said, we all need cheaper black jeans from time to time, and knowing how to prevent them from fading is key to an elevated outfit. According to stylist Sasha Izum, following the specific jeans' washing and drying instructions is key to denim longevity. But, she also has some preventative tips: “Do not choose charcoal off-black denim, as such jeans look like untrue blacks. Instead, choose slightly faded jeans, similar to shades of wet asphalt.” By choosing those richer black colors, your jeans will remain crisp and bright over time.
