FAQs

1. What is the storyline of “When the Wind Blows”?

“When the Wind Blows” follows the story of Jim and Hilda Bloggs, an elderly couple living in rural England. When news of an imminent nuclear attack reaches them, they follow government-issued guidelines to protect themselves from the fallout. The film depicts their struggles and the devastating consequences of nuclear war on their lives.

2. Is “When the Wind Blows” based on a book?

Yes, “When the Wind Blows” is based on a graphic novel of the same name by Raymond Briggs. The book was published in 1982 and gained critical acclaim for its powerful portrayal of the effects of nuclear war on civilians.

3. Who directed “When the Wind Blows”?

The film was directed by Jimmy T. Murakami, a Japanese-American animator and director known for his work in both animation and live-action films. His direction brings a unique blend of realism and animation to the movie.

4. What is the message of “When the Wind Blows”?

“When the Wind Blows” delivers a powerful anti-nuclear message, emphasizing the devastating consequences of nuclear war. It serves as a reminder of the importance of peace, diplomacy, and the need to protect our planet.

5. Is “When the Wind Blows” suitable for all audiences?
