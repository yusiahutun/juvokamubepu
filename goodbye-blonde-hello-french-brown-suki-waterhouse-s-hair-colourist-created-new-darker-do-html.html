Goodbye blonde, hello 'French brown': Suki Waterhouse's hair colourist on how he created her new dar

Suki Waterhouse turned heads more than usual with her new chestnut locks in London this weekend.

The Burberry model's new hue, believed to be for an upcoming film role, looked elegant, glossy and healthy peeking from a black beanie - and is sure to inspire countless copycats.

But, Fashionista.com reveals, this is not just any shade of brown. No, this is 'French Brown', and it is far more complicated than the standard salon dye job.

Not just any brunette: Model Suki Waterhouse's colourist calls her new hue 'French Brown'

Fair lady: Miss Waterhouse pictured with her formerly blonde hair earlier this month at a party in London

The man responsible for Miss Waterhouse's transformation is Jack Howard of London's Neville Salon.

He told the site that he used balayage to get the perfect shade, a popular technique that originated in France in the 1970s and requires the colourist to paint the hair freehand, rather than using foils.

'When going from blonde to brunette, you need to pre-colour before picking the right tone,' he explained. 

'When I balayage I like to do brown-on-brown, or what I call a "French Brown," with no more than three levels of lightness. If you don't pre-colour, the shade can go muddy or green, and it's also essential for getting an even finish. Then you rinse out the pre-colour, dry fully and apply the final chosen shade.'

He says balayage lends depth, dimension and texture to brunette hair.

London girls: Miss Waterhouse (left) pictured on Saturday night with Cara Delevingne (right) and Lauryn Hill (centre) at the Dover Street Arts Club

Miss Waterhouse, 22, who has been dating actor Bradley Cooper for the past 18 months, showed off her new look at a private Lauryn Hill gig at Dover Street Arts Club on Saturday night.

She was joined by fellow model Cara Delevingne, and the pair posed for photos with the singer inside the venue.

Later, they moved on to G-A-Y where they saw Jessie J perform live.
