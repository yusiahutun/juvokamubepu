Biography Timeline

Kimiko Glenn is an American actress and singer best known for her roles in Broadway productions, television series, and films. She was born on June 27, 1989, in Phoenix, Arizona, and started her acting career at a young age. Here is a detailed biography timeline of her life and career:

  • 2008: Glenn made her professional debut in the Off-Broadway production of “Walmartopia.”
  • 2011: She landed her first television role in the crime drama series “Law & Order: Special Victims Unit.”
  • 2013: Glenn made her Broadway debut in the musical “Waitress,” playing the role of Dawn.
  • 2014: She appeared in the film “Orange Is the New Black,” playing the recurring role of Brook Soso.
  • 2015: Glenn appeared in the film “We Need to Talk About Kevin,” playing the role of Celia.
  • 2016: She returned to Broadway in the musical “Paramour,” playing the lead female role of Indigo James.
  • 2017: Glenn played the role of Lady Liv in the Netflix animated series “Voltron: Legendary Defender.”
  • 2018: She starred in the Broadway production of “The Hardest Part of Love.”
  • 2019: Glenn starred in the Netflix series “The Guest Book.”
  • 2020: She starred in the Netflix limited series “Social Distance.”
  • 2021: Glenn played the role of Dawn in the Broadway production of “Waitress” once again.

Glenn has received critical acclaim throughout her career for performing in various productions. She has also strongly advocated for mental health awareness and LGBTQ+ rights. Glenn’s talent and dedication have made her a rising star in the entertainment industry.
