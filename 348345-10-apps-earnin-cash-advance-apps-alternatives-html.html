10 apps like Earnin: cash advance apps alternatives

The world economy has recently been sleepwalking into a new financial crisis, and sadly, no one is spared the brunt of the debacle, including the financially stable. According to various financial survey companies, more than 40% of adults are struggling to pay their rent and bills on time! This is one of the major reasons why cash advance apps like Earnin and Dave have become immensely important now than ever before in most parts of the world.

Earnin (formerly Activehours), is one of the most sought-after payday advance apps that helps you cash out up to $100 per day of the cash you have earned. The beauty of Earnin is, there is no fee or interest charge but you can choose what you want to pay to support the service. Earnin offers several features which include:

  • The Balance Shield: This safeguards you from substantial bank overdraft fees and helps you keep track of your bank balance. It also alerts you if your balance drops below a specific amount.
  • Cashback Rewards: The Earnin app also acts as a shopping reward app. You only need to link your credit or debit card to your Earnin account. If you opt to run it as a credit card when paying, you will be eligible to earn rewards of 1% to 10% cash back at your local stores or restaurants.

6. Brigit

This is one of the most flexible cash advance apps like Dave Reddit and apps like Earnin with no fees. Brigit provides cash advances of up to $250, which is sent to your account within one minute with no credit check required.

Like Earnin, Brigit notifies you when your account balance is running low so you can request an advance if you need it. It also allows you to set up automated advances that enable you to collect a transfer anytime your account balance is at risk of dropping below zero to avoid hefty bank overdraft fees.

However, unlike other apps like Earnin, Brigit has specific requirements which include:

  • Income: You have to prove that your monthly pay is above a certain amount, which they do not specify on the site. Moreover, you must have a single steady income source.
  • Banking: You are required to own a checking account that's at least 60 days old with a balance greater than $0. You also have to prove that you use your account nearly every day to make payments, receive deposits, or check balances.
  • Brigit Score: The app also features a scoring system that is dependent on your earnings, spending, and banking behaviour. The score can range from 1 to 100, and you should score at least 70 points to get any cash advances.

7. DailyPay

DailyPay is one of the most reliable payday loan apps that offers employees and contractors early access to the capital they have earned. For each day worked, you build up an accessible balance, which you can request at any time. The requesting fee is as low as $1.25 per request.

The app works like Earnin, and it does not cost employers anything. It also requires no changes to their existing payroll process. Companies that use it include Vera Bradley, Shiftgig, Westgate Resorts, and The Maids International.

8. PayActiv

PayActiv works in the same way as DailyPay, and its core feature is "earned wage access," or EWA, which is the ability to amass your wages as soon as you earn them.

It enables you to receive up to 50% of any wages you have already earned, up to a maximum of $500. It also allows you to transfer cash to your bank account and use it to pay bills online. The flexible cash advance app charges a flat fee of between $0 and $5, based on your employer's itinerary, each time you use it.

Branch is another payday advance app designed mainly for employers. It helps keep track of the staff: who is sick, who is on vacation, and who is working which shift. It also allows workers to request time off, swap shifts, and receive schedule reminders.

For employees, Branch offers financial tools to get ahead. It helps you:
