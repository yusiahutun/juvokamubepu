PICTURED: Divorced Florida man, 41, who shot daughter, 12, and son, 9, then killed himself at lake

A recently divorced father of two who is said to have suffered depression posted a photo of him and his two children just an hour before shooting dead his kids and then turning the gun on himself. 

Humberto Christian Tovar, 41, posted a smiling, happy picture of his two children Valeria, 12, and Matias, nine, and himself in the car on Facebook just under an hour before the fatal shooting in Miami Lakes, Florida, on Tuesday.

The kids' frantic mother came upon the horrific scene shortly after they were killed and tried to resuscitate the children - her screams reverberated throughout the normally quiet community. 

Tovar, an avid cyclist, was said to be suffering from bipolar depression after he and wife Jessica Forero Cardona divorced in August after 12 years together. 

'He was dealing with bipolarity. He didn't take his pills. He never took care of it. He never wanted to take medication therapy,' his mother, Luz Kuznitz, told Local 10 News. She said her son biked quite often to help with his mental illness and to relieve some of his stress. 

Tovar, who had been working at City Bikes in Aventura for six months, would often post pictures of him cycling on his Facebook page.  

More shockingly, Kuznitz had asked her son if he 'would ever be able to harm Valeria and Matias?' 

He told his mother at the time: 'No, never, absolutely not.'

'I am going to miss my grandchildren,' Kuznitz told Local 10 News. 

The children's mother found her children and ex-husband by a canal in Miami Lakes around 9pm on Tuesday. She could be heard screaming on a nearby Ring doorbell as she desperately tried to resuscitate her lifeless son. 

Cardona became worried when she hadn't heard from her children,  who had school the next day. Tovar had picked up the two children from the mother's home, according to Miami-Dade police. 

She then reportedly tracked her family to the lake at around 9pm on the 6400 block of Miami Lakes Drive, near a Publix supermarket. The mom found all three bodies on the grass next to a concrete bench on a section of the lake facing Miami Lakes Drive, according to police

The Miami-Dade Police Director Alfredo 'Freddy' Ramirez III confirmed the deaths on Twitter, writing: 'Earlier this evening, officers responded to the scene of a shooting where a father senselessly shot and killed his 12-year-old daughter and nine-year-old son before killing himself.' 

A GoFundMe has been set up for Cardona to help afford the funeral cost and mental healthcare she will need after 'such a devastating experience.' 

'Jessica did everything she could to save her children,' Kendaly Alvarez, who started the GoFundMe said. 'Now, she’s left with the aftermath and pain of such a devastating experience.' 

It has raised $1,585 of it's $25,000 goal as of Thursday morning.

A nearby resident Madga Peña, whose Ring doorbell picked up on the mother's cries, said she and her son started running toward the lake when they heard the woman in distress. 

'I just opened the door and I ran,' she told WSVN. 'My son ran behind me. He didn't even have shoes. I ran across the grass and when I got there I saw the lady like on top of the little boy. I couldn't see the dad or the daughter because of the darkness at first.' 

The community is a mixture of townhouse developments and commercial property. 

Police were  seen on the scene near the lake in Miami Lakes on Tuesday evening 

Vans and police cars lined up near the lake where the three bodies were found 

'I started looking and I saw the man's body there and I saw the little girl's body there, and the mom was trying to do compressions on the little girl, she even pulled her body towards the son's body to bring them closer and she herself started doing chest compressions on them,' Peña told NBC 6 Miami. 

'At that point, I was crying already, I think I went into shock cause I was just like freaked out, I don't know.'

Peña, who also helped with chest compressions, also said she saw the gun lying next to the father's body, leaving her in shock 'for a good hour.' 

'I think I cried for like an hour and I was just shaking and I came inside the house. I think I was up until like three o'clock in the morning because I was there looking at everything and wondering how this happened because this has never happened here.' 

Investigators were later seen looking under a tarp that covered a body on the scene. Police cruisers were also seen in a nearby Publix's parking lot. 

Police are reporting it as a 'domestic incident,' according to CBS 4 Miami. The motive has not been released.
