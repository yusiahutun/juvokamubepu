Robert 'Sandy' Vietze: JetBlue passenger who peed on sleeping girl revealed as ski champion

Shamed ski champion faces one year jail sentence for 'urinating on 11-year-old girl aboard JetBlue flight'

The 18-year-old champion skier charged with urinating on a young girl aboard a JetBlue flight to New York faces up to a year in jail for the alleged offence.

Robert 'Sandy' Vietze was dismissed from the U.S. Ski Team's development squad after he was charged with indecent exposure for urinating on a minor, a federal misdemeanour.

He was detained at John F Kennedy International Airport by Port Authority police, who say the charge carries a maximum punishment of a $1,000 fine and a year in jail.

Charged: Robert 'Sandy' Vietze had refused to apologize after the incident, where he allegedly unzipped and relieved himself on the 11-year-old girl

The career of the talented ski champ has gone downhill fast after he was identified as the passenger accused of unzipping and relieving himself on an 11-year-old girl on Wednesday.

In a statement issued on Friday, U.S. Ski and Snowboard Association Executive Vice President of Athletics Luke Bodensteiner said Mr Vietze has been dismissed from the team for conduct violations.

Mr Bodensteiner said: 'Based on the information we have, Sandy Vietze is in violation of the USSA code of conduct and team agreement, and has been dismissed from the team.'

Mr Vietze, 18, of South Warren, Vermont, had refused to apologize after the incident, reportedly telling authorities: 'I was drunk, and I did not realise I was p*****g on her leg.'

Meanwhile, the girl's family said in a statement on Friday Mr Vietze did not urinate on their daughter, but on the floor next to her seat, reports OregonLive.com.

A statement released by Hillsboro lawyer Robert Harris reads: 'While family members have cooperated with authorities in the investigation, they neither welcome nor encourage further publicity.'

The family has asked not to be identified.

'The family's main concern is the welfare of their daughter,' the statement read.

Mr Vietze was gunning for a chance to compete in the 2014 Winter Olympics in Russia, but his mid-flight 'accident' has cost him.

He was returning to Vermont after a week-long training with the U.S. Ski team at Oregon's Mount Hood.

Multiple calls to Mr Vietze's family home in Warren were not answered on Friday.

His mother, Abigail, told the New York Post: 'We have no comment, nothing to say.'

Despite the statement from the family of the girl allegedly involved in the incident, Robert Nardoza, a spokesman for the U.S. attorney's office in Brooklyn, New York, said Mr Vietze was slapped with a summons to appear in court at a later date.

A spokesperson for the Port Authority added: 'He was intoxicated and was charged, as it's against the law to pee on another person.'

According to a witness, the incident shocked the girl’s family, and flight attendants had to separate Mr Vietze and the girl’s irate father, a stage four cancer patient.

Flightmare! The incident occurred aboard JetBlue flight 166 to New York's John F Kennedy airport, where Mr Vietze was detained

The girl's father was reportedly using the toilet at the time, but returned to find Mr Veitze midstream.

One witness said: 'I woke up to this man yelling and literally looking like he was about to punch this kid in the face.'

'The father was screaming, "F**k that kid! I don't want him near my family!"' as flight attendants attempted to separate the pair.

The drunk teen was then removed to the back of the plane and attendants scurried to clean up the little girl.

As tensions rose aboard the flight, another passenger, complaining of chest-pains, began to vomit, prompting the pilot to ask: 'Is anybody on this flight a nurse or a doctor?’

The crew then cleaned up his mess as well.

Ski champion: Vietze was training for the Russia 2014 Olympics, but has been kicked off the U.S. ski team

Slope star: Vietze attended the Green Mountain Valley School, one of the top ski academies in the U.S.

School of champions: Mr Vietze recently graduated from the Green Mountain Valley School, a top ski academy in Vermont, which costs about $40,000 a year to attend

'The pilots kept coming out of the cockpit to talk to the flight crew about what was going on,' the witness told the Post.

'When we landed, the pilot warned us that police were coming.'

Six Port Authority police officers met the plane as it landed in Kennedy Airport at 6:30am - two for the sick passenger and four to escort Mr Vietze into custody.

When MailOnline contacted JetBlue they said only that an 'incident was reported on Flight 166' and would not say whether the little girl and her family would receive compensation or a free flight for their troubles.

Mr Vietze recently graduated from the Green Mountain Valley School, a top ski academy in Vermont, which costs about $40,000 a year to attend.

The date of Vietze's court appearance on federal charges was not immediately known, according to a spokesman for the U.S. Attorney's Office for the Eastern District of New York.
