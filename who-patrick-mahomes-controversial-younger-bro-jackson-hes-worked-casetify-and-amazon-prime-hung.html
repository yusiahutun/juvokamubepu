Jackson Mahomes grew up in Texas with his big bro, Patrick

Jackson Mahomes, 23, was born in Tyler, Texas to parents Pat Mahomes and Randi Martin, per People. Jackson and his brother Patrick share the same parents and are four years and seven months apart in age. They have a half-sister, Mia Randall.

What does Jackson Mahomes do for money?

Mahomes is an internet personality with nearly 250,000 followers on Instagram at the time of writing, meaning he makes bank off sponsored posts on his account.Per InTouch, he has over a million followers on TikTok and has collaborated with brands like Casetify and Amazon Prime. In a YouTube video, Mahomes spoke out about whether he uses his brother’s fame for “clout”. “Do I use him for clout? No … I mean … I don’t necessarily use him for clout, but there are a lot of advantages and I get a lot of opportunities by being his brother, so that’s pretty cool,” he explained.

He has been embroiled in multiple controversies

Jackson has found himself at the centre of multiple controversies over the years. In September 2021, a video of him pouring water on a Baltimore Ravens fan following a Chiefs loss went viral. He tweeted back in response, “They were thirsty.” His brother Patrick later told the Kansas City Star that the fan allegedly made comments towards Jackson and Patrick’s then-fiancée Brittany.

A month later, Jackson received heat for dancing during a tribute to late Washington player Sean Taylor, who died in 2007. He later apologised for the incident, tweeting, “I want to sincerely apologise for accidentally being on the Sean Taylor #21 at FedEx Field. We were directed to stand in that area and I meant absolutely no disrespect to him or his family.”

He’s been arrested

Jackson’s controversies don’t end there. In May this year, he was arrested on charges of aggravated sexual battery. He allegedly shoved a waiter at a restaurant in Kansas, then allegedly kissed the owner of the diner in her office without her consent, back in March. The Instagram star was later released from prison on a US$100,000 bond.
