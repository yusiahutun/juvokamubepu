How do I get a pilot’s certificate or license in Kenya?

The Private Pilot Certificate (internationally called the Private Pilot License (PPL)) is your foundation course for obtaining a Commercial Pilot’s License. Enroll at an aviation school and complete the following requirements:

  • Be at least 16 years for a license in a glider or balloon.
  • Be at least 17 years for a license other than the operation of glider or balloon.
  • Have a valid Class 2 Medical Certificate.
  • Receive an endorsement for a student pilot logbook's knowledge test in from an authorized instructor who: Trained you on the required aeronautical knowledge areas applying to the aircraft category you want and certified that you are ready for the knowledge test.
  • Pass the required PPL knowledge test.
  • Meet the aeronautical experience requirements of the aircraft category and class rating you want before applying for the practical test.
  • Receive flight training and a logbook endorsement from an authorized instructor who trained you on required areas of operation for the rating being sought and certified that you are ready for the practical test.
  • Pass a practical test - PPL (GFT) done by an authorized examiner.
  • Pass the English Language Proficiency Exam.
